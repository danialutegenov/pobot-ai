### Answer

Based solely on the provided context:

The rights of domestic workers in Hong Kong are primarily governed by the Employment Ordinance (Cap. 57). The specific rights depend on whether the domestic worker is employed under a "continuous contract."

**1. Basic Rights for All Employees (Including Domestic Workers):**
All employees covered by the Employment Ordinance, irrespective of their hours of work, are entitled to basic protections including:
*   Wage protection ("payment of wages, restrictions on wages deductions") [A Concise Guide to the Employment Ordinance, https://www.labour.gov.hk/eng/public/wcp/ConciseGuide/EO_guide_full.pdf].
*   Statutory holidays [Labour Department FAQ Index Topic 1, https://www.labour.gov.hk/eng/faq/cap57a_whole.htm].
*   Protection against anti-union discrimination [Labour Department FAQ Index Topic 1, https://www.labour.gov.hk/eng/faq/cap57a_whole.htm].

**2. Additional Rights for Employees on a Continuous Contract:**
Employees employed under a "continuous contract" are entitled to all statutory benefits under the Employment Ordinance, subject to meeting the conditions [Labour Department FAQ Index Topic 1, https://www.labour.gov.hk/eng/faq/cap57a_whole.htm]. These benefits include:
*   Rest days
*   Paid annual leave
*   Sickness allowance
*   Maternity protection
*   Statutory paternity leave
*   Severance payment
*   Long service payment
*   Provisions on termination of employment contract
[Labour Legislation Content 2, https://www.labour.gov.hk/eng/legislat/content2.htm].

A "continuous contract" requires being employed by the same employer for four weeks or more and meeting specific working hour requirements (e.g., working at least 17 hours per week) [A Concise Guide to the Employment Ordinance, https://www.labour.gov.hk/eng/public/wcp/ConciseGuide/EO_guide_full.pdf].

**3. Specific Information for Foreign Domestic Helpers (FDHs):**
A dedicated resource for Foreign Domestic Helpers states they are entitled to:
*   At least 1 rest day in every period of 7 days.
*   Statutory holidays and paid annual leave.
*   Full payment of wages as specified in the contract, not later than 7 days after the wage period.
*   Free food or a food allowance.
*   Free accommodation with reasonable privacy at the employer's residence.
*   Free medical treatment during employment.
*   Provision of travel to Hong Kong at the start of the contract and a return ticket upon completion/termination.
[FDH Corner, https://www.fdh.labour.gov.hk/en/fdh_corner.html].

**Important Application Note:**
The Employment Ordinance applies to all employees **except** for specific categories, including "a family member who lives in the same dwelling as the employer" [Labour Department FAQ Index Topic 1, https://www.labour.gov.hk/eng/faq/cap57a_whole.htm; A Concise Guide to the Employment Ordinance, https://www.labour.gov.hk/eng/public/wcp/ConciseGuide/EO_guide_full.pdf]. The provided context does not clarify if this exception applies to live-in domestic workers who are not family members.

For comprehensive details, you should refer to the full text of the Employment Ordinance and the specific handbook for Foreign Domestic Helpers cited in the sources.
